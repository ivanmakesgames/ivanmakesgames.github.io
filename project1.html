HeadHunter - Wild West Train Adventure

(Level design exercise)



Project Goal:

  • Goal: Create an action-adventure single-player FPS game prototype with a focus on level design.
  • Plot: The player is introduced to a stolen train full of murderers and bandits. At the front part of the fright train there is a wanted bandit who is standing behind a whole train robbery. The player must move forward from the back of the train to the front to arrest or eliminate the master bandit
  • Important: The game also has different endings based on the player's decisions, as well as the ability to play in a stealth mode.

⭐ What I did as a Level Designer
  • Designed the layout of the train levels and enviroment to create a believable and immersive experience;
  • Placed assets from Unity Asset Store and Sketchfab to create a unique and immersive environment;
  • Implemented gameplay mechanics from open-source github repository such as movement, jumping, shooting, vaulting, and ladder climbing to make levels challenging and engaging;
  • Created different game paths to allow players to approach levels in different ways;
  • Designed and implemented the train moving mechanics with C# to create a dynamic environment (you can find it in "Screenshots" section);
  • Implemented respawning feature: If player falls off the train he is being respawned at the beginning of the train.
⭐ Description

In HeadHunter, the player learns the basics of movement, jumping, shooting, vaulting, and ladder climbing in the first two wagons of the train. The game also introduces collectibles, health, and ammo mechanics, and allows the player to fight enemies using guns or melee attacks. The game can also be played in stealth mode, with different ways to avoid contact with enemies or loud fights.

HeadHunter's many endings are one of its distinguishing aspects. The player can reach a typical ending in which they kill all foes and apprehend the chief robber, but they must leap into a lake to live since they tripped the railway stop mechanisms. Instead, the player can reach a satisfying ending in which they not only kill all foes and apprehend the chief robber, but also release any captives and train any employees they locate. They must still leap from the train to survive, but the bandit is apprehended. Eventually, the player might have a horrible ending in which they do not apprehend the robber and the train crashes.
